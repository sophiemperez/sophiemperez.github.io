Case Study 01 — Frontera Capital Advisors

A 30-page report.
A 7-week plan.
Real results.

The Problem

Executive leadership wanted to release a 30-page M&A outlook report for Latin America. A single PDF drop generates one spike and goes quiet. The research deserved more reach than that.

The Strategy

I recommended restructuring it as a 6-part LinkedIn series — one chapter per week, each country as its own post. Built the release calendar, identified Tuesday as peak engagement day, planned the newsletter launch to close the series.

The Execution

Wrote and coordinated every post. Distributed across multiple team profiles to broaden organic reach. Tracked weekly. Adjusted hook language by country based on what resonated with the IB and PE audience.

The Project

Phia Music is my independent artist project — and the most complete marketing operation I run. I handle everything: content strategy, visual identity, DSP distribution across Spotify and Apple Music, press outreach, and community engagement. No label. No team. Just a plan and consistent execution.

Guitar cover reels targeting established fanbases — Deftones, QOTSA, Alice in Chains, Nirvana — with original music woven in. Case Study 03 — NYU Gender Equality in Music

Built the brand.
Grew the community.
350+ members.

The Context

I became President of NYU's Gender Equality in Music club — GEM — during COVID. The organization's mission is to create a safe space for students whose gender identity is underrepresented in the music industry. Taking it on during a pandemic meant the immediate challenge wasn't just leadership — it was keeping a community alive when there was no campus to build it on.

The Challenge

Socially distanced community-building is genuinely hard. No in-person events, no foot traffic, no organic word-of-mouth from seeing a booth or catching a show. Every member had to be reached deliberately — through content, outreach, and virtual programming — at a time when everyone was already experiencing screen fatigue. Growing a club in those conditions required the same instincts as any digital audience-building campaign: show up consistently, give people a reason to stay, and make the community feel real even when it wasn't physical.

The Comeback

Once campus reopened, I shifted to rebuilding in-person momentum — events, panels, the activities fair booth in the photo. I designed the GEM visual identity, ran the Instagram at @nyu.gem ↗, and coordinated panels with Aly & AJ, Mahalia, and Sarah Kinsely. By the time I finished my term the club had grown from 100 to 350+ members.
